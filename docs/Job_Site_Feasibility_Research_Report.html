Job Site Feasibility Research

Archilles Job Pipeline — June 2026 | Prepared by Jade (Hermes CLI Agent)

1. Executive Summary

We evaluated 11 job sites for automated discovery by Archilles, a Hermes agent running on a Hetzner VPS. Each site was tested for login requirements, anti-bot defenses, API availability, and headless browser viability. The goal: identify which sites Archilles can reliably scrape without legal risk or technical headaches.

Core Strategy

Many UK tech companies use Lever, Ashby, or Greenhouse as their Applicant Tracking System (ATS). These ATS platforms expose clean, unauthenticated APIs. Instead of fighting LinkedIn or Indeed's bot defenses, Archilles will: (1) discover companies via open sources (Hacker News, reed.co.uk), then (2) go directly to their ATS APIs for structured job data. Skip the middlemen entirely.

Tier 3 — Not Worth It

The cost of proxies, account management, and legal exposure outweighs any benefit — especially when Tier 1 already covers the same jobs via company ATS boards.

4. Why This Strategy Works

The key insight: LinkedIn and Indeed are middlemen, not sources. When a company posts a job, it ultimately lands on their ATS (Lever, Ashby, Greenhouse, or Workday). LinkedIn/Indeed just aggregate and wrap ads around it.

By discovering companies through open channels (HN monthly thread, reed.co.uk company names) and then hitting their ATS APIs directly, Archilles gets:

  • Structured data — JSON with title, location, description, department, apply URL
  • No auth — these ATS APIs are public by design (companies embed them on their careers pages)
  • No legal risk — public APIs, no ToS circumvention, no scraping behind login
  • No proxy costs — works from a plain Hetzner VPS with zero blocks

6. Company Discovery Pipeline

How Archilles finds companies to query

Step 1: Scrape HN "Who Is Hiring" monthly thread → extract company names from "London", "Remote UK", "UK" comments.
Step 2: Scrape reed.co.uk search results → extract company names from listing cards.
Step 3: For each company name, check: do they use Lever? Ashby? Greenhouse?
Step 4: If yes → query their ATS API. If no → skip or add to Workday queue.
Step 5: Deduplicate across sources by company+title fingerprint.

Note: LinkedIn and Indeed are blacklisted not because scraping is technically impossible, but because it's not worth it. The same jobs appear on company ATS boards (Tier 1) without any of the legal or technical overhead. For every hour we'd spend fighting Cloudflare and LinkedIn's legal team, we could have processed 200 jobs through clean APIs.

8. Verdict

4 whitelist sources (Lever, Ashby, Greenhouse, HN) provide clean, structured, legally-safe job data with zero scraping complexity. Combined with 4 graylist sources (reed, cwjobs, cord, Workday) for company discovery, Archilles can cover the UK Python/backend job market comprehensively without ever touching LinkedIn or Indeed.

The total implementation for Phase 1+2 is approximately 4 cron jobs and 150 lines of bash — no proxies, no captcha solvers, no account management. The smart play is spending engineering time on the evaluation pipeline and pack generation, not on fighting bot defenses.
