# HaxJobs Application Pack Standard

## Based on research from Prospects.ac.uk (May 2025), UK recruiter expectations, and ATS best practices.

## Pack Levels

### Level 1 — Quick Apply Pack (Score 80+)
**When:** Strong fit, auto-submit ready (Lever/Ashby/Greenhouse forms)
**Contains:**
- Tailored CV (PDF + Markdown) — 2 pages max A4, clean design
- Short cover letter (PDF + Markdown) — 3-5 paragraphs, one page
- Fit report (score breakdown, strongest matches, major gaps)
- Application URL
**Action:** Hermes can fill form, upload CV, pause for user approval before submit

### Level 2 — Standard Pack (Score 60-79)
**When:** Good fit, needs human judgment
**Contains:**
- Everything in Level 1
- Application Q&A (common questions + suggested answers)
- Interview prep notes (gap handling, likely follow-ups)
**Action:** User reviews then applies manually or asks Hermes to apply

### Level 3 — Lite Pack (Score 40-59)
**When:** Weak fit, low priority
**Contains:**
- Fit report only (score, gaps, URL)
- CV reuse suggestion — links to closest matching saved CV
**Action:** User decides whether to invest effort; no cover letter auto-generated

### Level 4 — Skip (Below 40)
**When:** Not a fit
**Contains:**
- Fit report only (skip reason)
**Action:** Nothing generated beyond the report

## CV Standards (from research)

### Structure (in order)
1. **Contact details** — name, location, phone, email, LinkedIn, GitHub. No DOB, no photo.
2. **Professional summary** — 3-4 lines, first-person, sector-focused (not job-specific — that's the cover letter's job)
3. **Core skills** — grouped by competency area, not a flat keyword dump
4. **Experience** — reverse chronological. Title, company, dates, 3-4 bullets each
5. **Selected projects** — 2-3 most relevant, with tech stack and impact
6. **Education** — most recent first, include qualification type and dates

### Writing rules
- **2 pages A4 maximum** (junior candidates: may be shorter)
- **Same font as cover letter** — consistent presentation
- **Bullet rhythm must vary** — mix short punchy bullets with longer detail bullets. Monotonous "Verb + [tech] + [purpose]" pattern is the #1 AI-generation signal
- **First-person summary** — "I'm a Python backend engineer..." not third-person
- **Use JD keywords naturally** — not keyword-stuffed, not obvious ATS-tailoring
- **Every claim interview-defensible** — don't exaggerate, don't invent
- **Cut anything that doesn't sell you** — if you're struggling to fit 2 pages, ask: "Does this line help me get the interview?"
- **No internal notes in submitted CV** — no "Still do not overclaim", no "Optional lines", no "Gaps", no fit scores

### Common mistakes to avoid
- Using AI to write the whole thing (recruiters can spot it)
- Keyword stuffing for ATS (reads like a robot wrote it)
- Including irrelevant personal details (DOB, marital status, photo)
- Unexplained gaps (address them briefly or restructure)
- Spelling and grammar errors (triple-check)
- Generic personal statements copied across applications

## Cover Letter Standards (from research)

### Structure (3-5 paragraphs, one A4 page)
1. **Opening** — role you're applying for, where you saw it, one-line hook about why you
2. **Why you** — 2-3 strongest matches backed by real examples, not just claims
3. **Why them** — mention the company by name, show you've researched them (not just copy-pasted)
4. **Gap handling** — strength-first: lead with what you bring, gap as honest aside
5. **Closing** — availability, enthusiasm, call to action

### Writing rules
- **Tailor to every organisation** — generic cover letters are obvious
- **Use keywords from the job advert** — shows you read it properly
- **Identify your USPs** — unique selling points, not generic "hard worker" claims
- **Include examples** — back claims with real evidence ("built X which resulted in Y")
- **Complement your CV, don't duplicate it** — the cover letter adds colour, the CV provides facts
- **Research the company** before writing — who reads it, company culture, industry, competitors
- **Save a copy** — you'll need to refer back if you get an interview

### Role-conditional hooks (Arinze's variants)
| Role Type | Lead With |
|---|---|
| AI Engineer | "building AI applications is what I already do every day" |
| Full Stack | "backend-first engineer contributing across the full product surface" |
| Backend | "systems people rely on every day, engineering quality bar high" |

## Common Application Questions (UK market)

Based on Prospects.ac.uk and UK recruiter patterns:

### Almost always asked
1. **Why do you want to work at [company]?** — Show research, not flattery
2. **Why this role?** — Connect your experience to their specific needs
3. **Tell us about yourself** — 60-second pitch: who you are, what you've built, what you want next
4. **What are your salary expectations?** — Flexible range, market-anchored
5. **When can you start?** — Immediately (for Arinze)

### Frequently asked
6. **Describe a technical project you're proud of** — STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result)
7. **How do you handle tight deadlines or changing requirements?** — Real example
8. **Tell us about a time you disagreed with a teammate** — Professional resolution
9. **What's your experience with [specific technology]?** — Honest depth, not inflated
10. **Where do you see yourself in 5 years?** — Growth, not fantasy

### Legal/HR (must answer carefully)
11. **Do you have the right to work in the UK?** — Student visa → Graduate Route, graduating June 2026
12. **Will you now or in the future require sponsorship?** — "May require sponsorship in the future, but not immediately"
13. **Do you have any disabilities we should accommodate?** — Optional, personal choice
