I think I would bring useful builder energy to the team: I care about readable code, I test properly, and I like doing the unglamorous bits that make software reliable.

{gap_note}

If the team wants someone who can learn quickly, build carefully, and still be normal to work with, I think I would be a strong person to speak with. Trust me, I would make a good teammate here, unless your applicant pool is full of absolute monsters.

Best,
Arinze

## Fit-level guidance
Strong fit: keep the confidence high. It is okay to sound like Arinze knows he belongs in the room.

Medium fit: keep the same personality, but make the gap honest. Example: `I have not had deep production time with {missing_tool} yet, so I will not pretend otherwise. But the surrounding engineering patterns are familiar, and learning it should be very manageable. I would probably be reading the docs while writing this, if we are being honest.`

Stretch fit: stay curious and self-aware. Do not apologize for the gap, and do not fake it either.
